# Lesson 4: Photosynthesis — Converting Light into Matter

Photosynthesis is the fundamental biochemical process that allows photosynthetic organisms (plants, algae, cyanobacteria) to convert **light energy** into **chemical energy** stored as carbohydrates. Without photosynthesis, virtually no complex life would be possible on Earth.

<SelfEval title="Photosynthesis" type="pre" />

## 1. General Equation and Localization

The overall equation of photosynthesis is:

> 6 CO₂ + 6 H₂O + **light energy** → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6 O₂

Photosynthesis occurs in **chloroplasts** — double-membrane organelles possessing a third internal membrane system, the **thylakoids**, organized into stacks called **grana**.

<MetaNote title="Chloroplast vs. Mitochondria Comparison">
  Chloroplasts and mitochondria share an endosymbiotic origin but work in opposite directions energetically: mitochondria **consume** glucose and **produce** ATP; chloroplasts **produce** glucose by **consuming** light energy.
</MetaNote>

## 2. Light Reactions (Photochemical Phase)

Light reactions occur in the **thylakoid membranes** and capture light energy to produce ATP and NADPH.

### 2.1 Photosystems

Chloroplasts have two photosystems (pigment-protein complexes):

- **Photosystem II (PS II)**: Reaction center P680 (absorbs at 680 nm). Uses light energy to oxidize water: **2 H₂O → O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻**. This is the source of atmospheric oxygen.
- **Photosystem I (PS I)**: Reaction center P700 (absorbs at 700 nm). Reduces NADP⁺ to NADPH.

### 2.2 Electron Transport

Electrons flow from PS II to PS I via a transport chain (plastoquinone, b6f complex, plastocyanin), pumping H⁺ into the thylakoid lumen — creating a gradient that allows **chloroplastic ATP synthase (CF₀CF₁)** to synthesize ATP.

**Light reaction balance (for 2 water molecules):**
- 🟢 Produced: 3 ATP + 2 NADPH + 1 O₂
- 🔴 Consumed: Light energy

## 3. Carbon Fixation Phase (Calvin Cycle)

The **Calvin cycle** occurs in the **stroma** of the chloroplast and uses the ATP and NADPH produced during light reactions to fix atmospheric CO₂ into carbohydrates.

### 3.1 The 3 Stages of the Cycle

**Stage 1 — Carboxylation:**
- The enzyme **RuBisCO** (ribulose-1,5-bisphosphate carboxylase/oxygenase) fixes CO₂ onto **RuBP** (ribulose-1,5-bisphosphate, 5C) → 2 molecules of **3-phosphoglycerate (3-PGA, 3C)**

**Stage 2 — Reduction:**
- 3-PGA is reduced to **glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate (G3P)** using **ATP + NADPH**
- G3P is the precursor of all carbohydrates (glucose, sucrose, starch)

**Stage 3 — RuBP Regeneration:**
- Some G3P is used to regenerate **RuBP** (CO₂ acceptor) using **ATP**

**Balance for fixing 3 CO₂ (= 1 complete turn):**
- 🔴 Consumed: 3 CO₂ + 9 ATP + 6 NADPH
- 🟢 Produced: 1 net G3P (3C) + 3 regenerated RuBP

<MetaNote title="RuBisCO: The Most Abundant Enzyme in the Biosphere">
  RuBisCO is the most abundant enzyme on Earth (~0.7 billion tonnes). It inefficiently fixes CO₂ because it can also react with O₂ (photorespiration), a reaction that reduces photosynthetic efficiency. C4 plants (corn, sugarcane) have evolved mechanisms to concentrate CO₂ around RuBisCO and avoid this problem.
</MetaNote>
