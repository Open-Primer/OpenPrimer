# Lesson 3: Glycolysis and Cellular Respiration

Energy is the universal currency of life. A cell can neither store large amounts of ATP nor function long without producing it. **Aerobic cellular respiration** — glycolysis, the Krebs cycle, and oxidative phosphorylation — is the primary mechanism by which cells extract chemical energy from organic substrates.

<SelfEval title="Glycolysis and Cellular Respiration" type="pre" />

## 1. Energy Overview: ATP as Currency

**ATP (Adenosine TriPhosphate)** is the universal energy currency of the cell. Hydrolysis of one ATP to ADP + Pᵢ releases **~30.5 kJ/mol** under standard conditions (but more in real cellular conditions, ~50 kJ/mol).

The complete oxidation of one **glucose** molecule (C₆H₁₂O₆):

> C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O + **~30-32 ATP** (theoretical: 36-38 ATP)

releases approximately **2870 kJ/mol** of glucose.

<MetaNote title="Why Not 36-38 ATP in Practice?">
  The H⁺/ATP ratio of ATP synthase (~2.7 H⁺/ATP) and the transport costs of mitochondrial substrates reduce the theoretical yield. In practice, approximately 30-32 ATP per glucose is counted in mammalian cells.
</MetaNote>

## 2. Glycolysis: Phase 1 of Glucose Catabolism

Glycolysis occurs in the **cytosol** and converts one glucose molecule (6C) into two **pyruvate** molecules (3C). It comprises **10 enzymatic reactions** divided into two phases:

### 2.1 Investment Phase (Reactions 1-5)

The cell **consumes 2 ATP** to "activate" glucose:

1. **Glucose → Glucose-6-phosphate** (hexokinase/glucokinase, ATP → ADP)
2. **G6P → Fructose-6-phosphate** (phosphoglucose isomerase)
3. **F6P → Fructose-1,6-bisphosphate** (phosphofructokinase-1 [PFK-1], ATP → ADP) ← **Key regulatory step**
4. **F1,6-bisP → Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate + DHAP** (aldolase)
5. **DHAP → G3P** (triose phosphate isomerase)

> After reaction 4, we have **2 G3P molecules** (triose phosphate) per glucose.

### 2.2 Recovery Phase (Reactions 6-10)

The cell **produces 4 ATP and 2 NADH** per glucose:

6. **G3P → 1,3-bisphosphoglycerate** (G3P dehydrogenase, NAD⁺ → NADH) × 2
7. **1,3-bisP → 3-phosphoglycerate** (phosphoglycerate kinase, ADP → ATP) × 2 ← **Substrate-level phosphorylation**
8. **3-PG → 2-phosphoglycerate** (phosphoglycerate mutase) × 2
9. **2-PG → Phosphoenolpyruvate (PEP)** (enolase) × 2
10. **PEP → Pyruvate** (pyruvate kinase, ADP → ATP) × 2 ← **Substrate-level phosphorylation**

**Net glycolysis balance per glucose molecule:**
- 🔴 Consumed: 2 ATP
- 🟢 Produced: 4 ATP + 2 NADH + 2 Pyruvate
- ✅ **Net gain: 2 ATP + 2 NADH + 2 Pyruvate**

### 2.3 Glycolysis Regulation

**PFK-1 (phosphofructokinase-1)** is the key regulatory enzyme (step 3):

| Signal | Effect on PFK-1 | Meaning |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| High ATP | Allosteric inhibition | Cell has enough energy |
| High AMP/ADP | Activation | Cell is energy-depleted |
| High citrate | Inhibition | Krebs cycle is saturated |
| Fructose-2,6-bisphosphate | Potent activation | Insulin signaling |

## 3. Pyruvate: The Metabolic Crossroads

The fate of pyruvate depends on oxygen availability:

### 3.1 Under Anaerobic Conditions: Fermentation

| Organism/Tissue | Reaction | Product | Utility |
|-----------------|---------|---------|---------|
| Yeasts | Pyruvate → Ethanol + CO₂ | Ethanol | Alcoholic fermentation (winemaking) |
| Muscle under intense effort | Pyruvate → Lactate | Lactate | Regenerates NAD⁺ to continue glycolysis |
| Lactic acid bacteria | Pyruvate → Lactate | Lactate | Lactic fermentation (yogurt) |

### 3.2 Under Aerobic Conditions: Oxidative Decarboxylation

In the presence of O₂, pyruvate enters the **mitochondria** and is converted by the **pyruvate dehydrogenase complex (PDH)**:

> Pyruvate + CoA-SH + NAD⁺ → Acetyl-CoA + CO₂ + NADH

**Acetyl-CoA** (2C) is the entry substrate of the Krebs cycle.

## 4. The Krebs Cycle (Citric Acid Cycle)

The Krebs cycle occurs in the **mitochondrial matrix** and completely oxidizes acetyl-CoA to CO₂, producing reduced coenzymes (NADH, FADH₂) that will feed the respiratory chain.

**Balance per cycle turn (= 1 Acetyl-CoA entering):**
- 🔴 Entering: 1 Acetyl-CoA (2C)
- 🟢 Produced: 3 NADH + 1 FADH₂ + 1 GTP (≡ ATP) + 2 CO₂
- ⚠️ For 1 glucose: **2 turns** → 6 NADH + 2 FADH₂ + 2 GTP

The **8 cycle reactions** constantly regenerate oxaloacetate (OAA, 4C) which serves as acceptor for the next Acetyl-CoA.

<MetaNote title="Key Regulatory Enzymes of the Krebs Cycle">
  Three key enzymes negatively regulated by high ATP/NADH: **citrate synthase**, **isocitrate dehydrogenase**, and **α-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase**. When energy is deficient (high ADP/AMP), they are activated.
</MetaNote>

## 5. Oxidative Phosphorylation: Massive ATP Production

Oxidative phosphorylation occurs on the **inner mitochondrial membrane** and couples electron transfer from NADH/FADH₂ to O₂ with ATP synthesis.

### 5.1 The Electron Transport Chain (ETC)

The ETC consists of **4 transmembrane complexes** (I, II, III, IV) and two mobile carriers (ubiquinone/CoQ, cytochrome c):

1. **Complex I (NADH dehydrogenase)**: NADH → CoQ, pumps **4 H⁺** into IMS
2. **Complex II (Succinate dehydrogenase)**: FADH₂ → CoQ, **0 H⁺** pumped
3. **Complex III (Cytochrome bc₁)**: CoQ → Cyt c, pumps **4 H⁺** into IMS
4. **Complex IV (Cytochrome c oxidase)**: Cyt c → O₂ (→ H₂O), pumps **2 H⁺** into IMS

**For 1 NADH**: 10 H⁺ pumped → ~2.5 ATP
**For 1 FADH₂**: 6 H⁺ pumped → ~1.5 ATP

### 5.2 ATP Synthase (Complex V): The Rotary Machine

**ATP synthase** (F₀F₁-ATPase) is a molecular nanomotor that couples the backflow of H⁺ into the matrix (following the electrochemical gradient) to ATP synthesis:

> ADP + Pᵢ + **~2.7 H⁺** → ATP + H₂O

Rotation of the F₀ c-subunit ring (driven by proton flux) drives rotation of the F₁ γ-subunit, inducing successive conformational changes in the 3 β-subunits that synthesize ATP.

## 6. Overall Energy Balance of Aerobic Respiration

| Step | Location | Direct ATP | NADH | FADH₂ | Total ATP (theoretical) |
|------|----------|-----------|------|-------|------------------------|
| Glycolysis | Cytosol | 2 | 2 | — | 2 + (2×2.5) = 7 |
| Oxidative decarboxylation | Mito. matrix | — | 2 | — | 2×2.5 = 5 |
| Krebs cycle | Mito. matrix | 2 | 6 | 2 | 2 + (6×2.5) + (2×1.5) = 20 |
| **TOTAL** | | **4 direct** | **10** | **2** | **~30-32 ATP** |
