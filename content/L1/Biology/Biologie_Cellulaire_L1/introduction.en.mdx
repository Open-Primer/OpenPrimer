# Introduction to Cell Biology: The Foundation of Life

Welcome to the **Cell Biology** L1 course. This course forms the indispensable cornerstone of all life sciences. To understand the cell is to understand life itself — from the microscopic bacterium to the complex human organism.

<SelfEval title="Introduction to Cell Biology" type="pre" />

## 1. Why Study the Cell?

The cell is the **fundamental unit of life**. All life as we know it is organized into cells. This theory, formulated in the 19th century by Schleiden, Schwann, and Virchow, remains one of the key conceptual pillars of modern biology.

<MetaNote title="Cell Theory — The 3 Core Postulates">
  1. All living organisms are composed of one or more cells.
  2. The cell is the structural and functional unit of life.
  3. All cells arise from pre-existing cells (omnis cellula e cellula).
</MetaNote>

## 2. Course Structure

This course is structured into two complementary major modules:

- **Module 1 — Cellular Architecture**: Plasma membrane, organelles, cytoskeleton. You will learn to distinguish prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells, and understand how each cellular compartment contributes to cell physiology.

- **Module 2 — Cellular Metabolism**: Glycolysis, the Krebs cycle, oxidative phosphorylation, and photosynthesis. You will understand how cells produce and consume the energy required for survival.

## 3. Prerequisites and Expected Level

This course is intended for first-year undergraduate science students (L1). A working knowledge of elementary organic chemistry (covalent bonds, functional groups) and basic physics (thermodynamics) will greatly facilitate learning.

<MetaNote title="Cross-Disciplinary Connections">
  Cell biology is intrinsically linked to chemistry (macromolecular biochemistry), physics (membrane biophysics), and mathematics (enzyme kinetics, modeling).
</MetaNote>
