# Introduction to Microbiology: The Invisible World

Welcome to the **Microbiology** L1 course. Micro-organisms — bacteria, viruses, fungi, protists, archaea — constitute the dominant living biomass of our planet and govern processes essential to life: biogeochemical cycles, immunity, biotechnology, and health.

<SelfEval title="Introduction to Microbiology" type="pre" />

## 1. Microbial Ubiquity

There are more bacterial cells in and on a human body than human cells themselves (~3.8×10¹³ bacteria for ~3×10¹³ human cells). The gut microbiome alone plays an essential role in digestion, immunity, and even neurology.

<MetaNote title="The Metagenomic Revolution">
  Before metagenomic sequencing (sequencing DNA directly collected from the environment), more than **99%** of micro-organisms were unculturable and unknown. Metagenomics revolutionized our understanding of microbial diversity — we discovered entire domains of life (halophilic and thermophilic archaea, DPANN).
</MetaNote>

## 2. Course Structure

- **Module 1 — Bacteria**: Morphology (cocci, bacilli, spirilla), cell wall (Gram+ vs Gram−), bacterial genetics (plasmids, conjugation, transduction, transformation), regulation (lac operon, trp operon).

- **Module 2 — Viruses**: Lysogenic and lytic cycles, structure (capsid, envelope), viral replication mechanisms (DNA, RNA, retroviruses), host-pathogen interactions.

- **Module 3 — Applied Microbiology**: Antibiotics and resistance mechanisms, biotechnologies (fermentation, genetic engineering), microbiome and health.

## 3. Classification of Micro-Organisms

| Group | Domain | Size | Characteristics |
|-------|--------|------|----------------|
| **Bacteria** | Bacteria | 1-10 μm | Prokaryotes, peptidoglycan |
| **Archaea** | Archaea | 0.5-10 μm | Prokaryotes, extreme environments |
| **Viruses** | (Acellular) | 20-300 nm | Obligate intracellular parasites |
| **Fungi** | Eukarya | 2-100+ μm | Eukaryotes, chitin cell wall |
| **Protists** | Eukarya | variable | Unicellular eukaryotes |
