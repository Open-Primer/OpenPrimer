# Introduction to Cell Biology

Cell biology is the study of life at its most fundamental level. As stated by the <Glossary>cell theory</Glossary> (founded by Schleiden, Schwann, and Virchow), the cell is the structural, functional, and reproductive unit of all living things. 

> "Omnis cellula e cellula" — All cells come from pre-existing cells.

## 1. The Cell Theory
To understand life, one must accept three fundamental principles:
1. **Structural Unity**: All living organisms are composed of one or more cells.
2. **Functional Unity**: The cell is the site of all metabolic reactions necessary for life.
3. **Reproductive Unity**: All cells arise from pre-existing cells through division.

## 2. Prokaryotes vs Eukaryotes
The cellular world is divided into two main categories based on the organization of their genetic material.

### <Glossary word="prokaryote">Prokaryotes</Glossary> (Bacteria, Archaea)
- **Size**: Small (0.1 to 5 μm).
- **Nucleus**: Absent (DNA is located in the nucleoid).
- **Organelles**: Lack of membrane-bound compartmentation.

### <Glossary word="eukaryote">Eukaryotes</Glossary> (Animals, Plants, Fungi)
- **Size**: Larger (10 to 100 μm).
- **Nucleus**: Present, surrounded by a nuclear envelope.
- **Organelles**: Presence of specialized compartments (Mitochondria, Golgi, ER).

---

## 3. Study Techniques
We cannot see a cell with the naked eye. Biologists use two main tools:
- **Optical Microscopy (OM)**: Uses light, allowing for the visualization of living cells but with limited resolution.
- **Electron Microscopy (EM)**: Uses electron beams for atomic resolution, revealing the ultrastructure of organelles.

## 📚 Glossary
- **Cytosol**: The liquid phase of the cytoplasm where organelles are suspended.
- **Organelle**: An intracellular compartment bounded by a membrane and performing a specific function.
- **DNA**: The molecule that carries genetic information.
