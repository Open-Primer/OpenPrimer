# Lesson 3: Transcription and Translation — Gene Expression

Gene expression is the process by which information encoded in a DNA sequence is converted into functional protein. This two-step process — **transcription** (DNA → RNA) then **translation** (RNA → Protein) — is the molecular manifestation of the Central Dogma.

<SelfEval title="Transcription and Translation" type="pre" />

## 1. Transcription: From DNA to mRNA

### 1.1 RNA Polymerase: The Transcription Engine

Unlike replication, transcription requires **no primer**: **RNA polymerase** directly initiates RNA synthesis from a free end. It synthesizes RNA in the **5'→3'** direction, using the template strand (antisense, 3'→5') as a template.

In eukaryotes, three major RNA polymerases:
- **RNA Pol I**: synthesizes rRNAs (except 5S)
- **RNA Pol II**: synthesizes mRNAs (proteins) and small RNAs
- **RNA Pol III**: synthesizes tRNAs, 5S rRNA

### 1.2 Steps of Transcription (Eukaryotes)

**Initiation:**
RNA Pol II is recruited to the **promoter** (region rich in TATA box, ~25-30 bp upstream of +1) by general transcription factors (TFIID, TFIIA, TFIIB, TFIIE, TFIIF, TFIIH) forming the **pre-initiation complex (PIC)**. TFIIH has kinase activity that phosphorylates the **C-terminal domain (CTD)** of RNA Pol II, signaling the transition to elongation mode.

**Elongation:**
RNA Pol II advances along the template strand at ~20-100 nt/s, creating a **transcription bubble** (~12-14 nt). The nascent RNA is released into the bubble lumen.

**Termination:**
In eukaryotes, termination involves the **AAUAAA** signal in the mRNA → cleavage and polyadenylation by the CPSF/CstF complex.

### 1.3 Maturation of Pre-Messenger RNA

Eukaryotic pre-mRNA undergoes three major modifications before being exported:

1. **7-methylguanosine cap (m7G)** at 5': added co-transcriptionally, protects against exonucleases and facilitates ribosome recognition
2. **Poly(A) tail** at 3': ~200 adenosines added by poly(A) polymerase, stabilizes mRNA and promotes nuclear export
3. **Splicing**: excision of **introns** (noncoding) and joining of **exons** (coding) by the **spliceosome** (ribonucleoprotein complex of ~5 snRNPs: U1, U2, U4, U5, U6)

<MetaNote title="Alternative Splicing: One Gene, Multiple Proteins">
  Alternative splicing allows a single gene to produce several different proteins depending on tissue or physiological condition. The **cardiac troponin T** gene produces more than 60 isoforms by alternative splicing. Approximately **95%** of human genes with multiple introns undergo alternative splicing.
</MetaNote>

## 2. The Genetic Code

The genetic code is the correspondence system between **codons** (mRNA base triplets) and the **amino acids** (or stop signals) they encode.

**Properties of the genetic code:**
- **Universal**: identical (with rare exceptions) in all living organisms
- **Non-overlapping**: each base belongs to a single codon
- **Degenerate (redundant)**: 64 codons for 20 amino acids + 3 stop codons → multiple codons encode the same amino acid (synonymous)
- **Unambiguous**: a given codon always encodes the same amino acid

**Special codons:**
- **AUG**: initiation codon (Met) — **always the first translated codon**
- **UAA, UAG, UGA**: stop codons (nonsense) — no amino acid

## 3. Translation: From mRNA to Protein

### 3.1 tRNAs: Adaptors of the Code

**Transfer RNAs (tRNAs)** are the molecular adaptors linking codons and amino acids:
- Each tRNA has an **anticodon** (loop that pairs with the mRNA codon)
- Each tRNA is charged with its specific amino acid by an **aminoacyl-tRNA synthetase** (aa-RS) — translation fidelity depends on these enzymes

### 3.2 The Ribosome: The Translation Machine

The eukaryotic ribosome (80S) is composed of:
- **Small subunit 40S**: decodes mRNA, binds translation initiation
- **Large subunit 60S**: catalyzes peptide bond formation (**peptidyltransferase** activity carried by 28S rRNA)

The ribosome has three functional sites for tRNAs:
- **A site (Aminoacyl)**: accepts incoming charged tRNA
- **P site (Peptidyl)**: holds tRNA carrying the growing peptide chain
- **E site (Exit)**: discharged tRNA, ready to leave

### 3.3 Steps of Translation

**Initiation:**
1. The 40S subunit, associated with eIF factors (eIF1, eIF2, eIF3, eIF4F), scans the mRNA from the 5' cap to the first **AUG** in optimal Kozak context
2. Initiator tRNA (Met-tRNA_i^Met) pairs with AUG at the P site
3. The 60S subunit joins → formation of functional 80S ribosome

**Elongation:**
For each amino acid added:
1. An aminoacyl-tRNA enters the A site (with eEF1A·GTP)
2. Peptidyltransferase catalyzes peptide bond formation (A site → P site)
3. Translocation moves the ribosome 3 nt toward 3' (eEF2·GTP)

**Termination:**
A stop codon at the A site is recognized by **release factors** (eRF1 + eRF3) → hydrolysis of the ester bond between peptide and last tRNA → protein release.
