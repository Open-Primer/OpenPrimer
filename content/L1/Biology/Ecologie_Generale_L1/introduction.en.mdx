# Introduction to General Ecology: Life and Its Environment

Welcome to the **General Ecology** L1 course. Ecology is the science that studies the interactions of living organisms with each other and with their physical environment. In the era of climate crisis and biodiversity collapse, it is one of the most urgent and relevant disciplines of our time.

<SelfEval title="Introduction to General Ecology" type="pre" />

## 1. Definitions and Levels of Organization

Ecology operates at several nested levels of organization:

| Level | Definition | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Individual** | Single organism | A lion, an oak tree |
| **Population** | All individuals of the same species in a space | Lions of the Serengeti |
| **Community (Biocenosis)** | All populations in a habitat | Fauna and flora of a tropical forest |
| **Ecosystem** | Biocenosis + Biotope (physical environment) | The Amazon rainforest |
| **Biome** | Set of ecosystems with similar climate | Arctic tundra |
| **Biosphere** | All inhabited zones of Earth | Earth |

## 2. Course Structure

- **Module 1 — Population Ecology**: Population dynamics (logistic and exponential models, survivorship curves), intra- and interspecific interactions (competition, predation, parasitism, mutualism), ecological niches.

- **Module 2 — Community and Ecosystem Ecology**: Food webs, energy flow (primary and secondary productivity), biogeochemical cycles (carbon, nitrogen, phosphorus), ecological successions.

- **Module 3 — Conservation and Global Change**: Climate change impacts on biodiversity, conservation biology, ecosystem services, sixth mass extinction.

## 3. The Biodiversity Crisis

<MetaNote title="Current Extinction Rate">
  The current extinction rate is estimated at **100 to 1000 times** the natural background extinction rate. We are living through the **sixth mass extinction** — the first caused by a single species (Homo sapiens). Since 1970, wild vertebrate populations have declined on average by **69%** (Living Planet Report 2022, WWF).
</MetaNote>
